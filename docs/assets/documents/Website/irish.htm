RUDDY - MURPHY - GARRAHY

From Ireland they came,  the Ruddy's and Murphy's arriving to Fairview Township, Allamakee County, Iowa as the Garrahy's were arriving to Wellington Township near the town of Fairfax in Renville County, Minnesota

Beginning in 1870's the second and third generations of
the Ruddy and Murphy families began leaving their farms in Iowa.


Ruddy and Murphy families moved to Fairfax and some to Chicago and Salt Lake City.

Garrahy's moved from Fairfax to Chicago

Some Murphy's moved from Fairfax to Ottertail County, Minnesota, Box Elder, Montana, and Saskatchewan, Canada

These three families all came from Ireland during the Famine years.

The Ruddy's: James, Michael, Thomas and (probably) Nicholas came from, maybe, Oughaval Roman Catholic parish in Co. Mayo during the 1840's. Recent research unearthed a document saying that Michael and Catherine were born in Clifton, Ireland [perhaps this meant Clifden, County Galway about 35 miles southwest of Westport where Michael and Catherine were married]. To this point I am sceptical that the family originated in Clifden, County Galway based on previous research. Another enclave of Ruddy's existed about 30 miles North and a bit West in Kilfian Parish ** where an instance of the rare name Nicholas Ruddy is also found Although the pronunciation of Clifton and Kilfian are far apart, one should not discount family lore*. Michael and Catherine went on to Liverpool and then on to New York City then to Syracuse, NY and, finally, they migrated to Iowa to join James Ruddy in Fairview Township. James Ruddy showed up in Iowa at the death of Thomas and married Ellen Murphy in Prairie de Chein. James Ruddy and his family moved by covered wagon in 1871-1872 to Wellington township in Renville County, Minnesota near the town of Fairfax.

The Murphy's emigrated from Co Limerick about 1850, stopped in Boston and then also migrated to Iowa. 

The Garrahy's were some of the first arrivals in Wellington Township migrating there in 1868 from Co Clare, Ireland by way of England and Massachusetts.

These families intermarried in Iowa and Minnesota.
