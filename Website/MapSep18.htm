September 18th 1863

Note: This map is one of a collection of 10 maps of the battle of Chickamauga which can be accessed and looked at in depth at the Library of Congress American Memory Collection. With that series of maps you can follow the progress of the battle hour by hour.

In the upper left center of the map Minty is shown back across Reed's Bridge where the brigade had retreated from Pea Vine Valley in front of Bushrod Johnson's Division. To the left of Minty at Dyer's Bridge are the men loaned to Minty by Wilder.

Wilder's main force can be seen in the center of the picture at Alexander's Bridge.

Thomas' Union troops can be seen heading for the area where Minty and Wilder are in the evening of the 18th.

The rest of the Union Army is off the map at the bottom right. It is not hard to imagine what would have happened if Minty and Wilder had fallen back and Bushrod Johnson, followed by Hood and Longstreet had fallen on the left flank of the Union lines before Thomas troops reached the area.

[Part of Map A at Chickamauga in the Library of Congress American Memory Collection]
