Update posted February 20, 2011

The Surname Romaguera is found principally in Catalunya, Valencia, Andorra, the Balearic Islands and in southeastern France where it borders on Catalunya.

In the 1800s there was an exodus out of Catalunya to the new world and there are Romaguera families found in the US, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Cuba and South America. I began a search for my wife's Romaguera ancestors and accumlated a lot of information on disconnected Romaguera families in the process. I am posting this information in hopes it may help someone who is searching that surname.

 

..... 1  SEARCHING ROMAGUERA

 The following "descendant ordered" report was constructed from my genealogy software database. In order to keep from having unconnected Romaguera families scattered throughout the database I collected unconnected families under an invented ‘ancestor’ called SEARCHING ROMAGUERA and then grouped the Romaguera families under other invented ‘sons’ named as the area where the Catalunyan Romaguera ancestor families were found in the world. These locations appear as if they were the second generation under the number “2”.

The number “1” ancestor, called SEARCHING ROMAGUERA, becomes a report place mark for all Romaguera locations.

The numbers “2” ancestors, “sons” of Searcing Romaguera represent the LOCATION or ORIGIN of various unconnected Romaguera families. Some are early Romaguera families within Catalunya and others originated in the new world. The descendant families, all begin with the number “3” under the location where the father of each Romaguera family is given and the family members then descends from the individual numbered “3” as generations “4, 5 , 6 etc.”  I have left off all descendants which may still be alive. In a few cases, where the person seemed to be related in some way at a given location, I have included persons with a surname other than Romaguera.

KEY POINTS TO REMEMBER

The Romaguera families beginning with “3” are not connected to each other but spring from the place where they were found in my research.

Numbering:

The number “2” is used for location of unconnected families in a specific area

The number “3” is used for the first known individual in the tree. The other numbers “4, 5, 6, etc.” are descendant-ordered from the individual numbered “3”

I have tried to be as accurate as I can, but in some cases I have GUESSED AT CONNECTIONS based on genealogical probability and connected some people and this will lead to errors. If an error is found email me and I will correct it. Also email me if you wish more information on Puerto Rico Romagueras and Louisiana Romagueras as I do have documentation collected on many families from these locations. The rest of this database was built using a collection of LDS Southwestern Europe disks, also, I have incorporated online databases from Catalunyan websites and genealogy websites of other people therefore I will not be able to provide any information for those families.

In general I have tried to use Catalán names but because of political and other reasons the names may be in Spanish or English. Many immigrants who went to Lousiana converted their names to English upon arrival or later after using their Catalán name for a time. There are also occasions where a name was entered into Catholic records in Latin or pseudo Latin by priests. Here are a few common equivalents:
