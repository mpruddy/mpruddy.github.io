INÉS CAMACHO ALBUM SECTION II
Plates 5-10

 

Plate 5
"El Mesías" de Los Chancos + [Generic photo]
Note: "El Mesias" was a mystic visionary, who was adopted as a good omen by the Conservative army , when he predicted 
God was on their side at the battle of Los Chancos July 31, 1876 during one of Colombia's many civil wars fought between Liberals 
and Conservatives. In spite of "El Mesias" shouting religious encouragement during the battle, the Liberals won the Battle 
of Los Chancos thanks to the confusion that ensued when the Conservative army fired on its own reinforcements. 

Note: It is doubtful that there is any connection between this man and Inés Camacho de Vallejo who was a staunch Liberal so the inclusion in
the album is mystifying. Perhaps Inés may have believed he really was religiously significant or was captivated by the story.

Plate 7
Gral. Tomás C. De Mosquera + Gral. Carral
Note: Written on the back of Mosquera's photo was written "El Gran General de la union Colombiana para el album de Inesita Camachor de V."
Note: General Tomás Cipriano Mosquera y Arboleda was a commander of the Liberal forces and was four times president of Colombia.
Note: General Leonardo Canal-González was a General for the Conservatives and was defeated by General Cipriano de Mosquera in 1862

 

Plate 8
Condesa Srta Mendeburre + Fidel Sinisterra
Note: Relationship of Condesa Srta Mendeburre, if any, to Inés Camacho is not known. 
Note: on the back of the photo of Fidel Sinistera is written "Retrato de me distinguido 
amigo Fidel Sinisterra, falleció el 13 de Abril de 1866" [see plate 9]

 

Plate 9
Dr. Fidel Sinisterra + Otelo y Noemi [generic photo]
Note: on the back of Sinisterra photo is written "A mi sra. Ines C. de Vallejo. Su atento estimador i leal amigo F. Sinisterra"
Note: In 1910 there was a Fidel Sinisterra listed on the newly created Cámera de Comericio [Chamber of Commerce] for Calí, 
but whether it is the same Fidel Sinisterra or not is unknown.

 

Plate 10
Jorge Fletcher + Joaquín Mesa
Note: on the back of the photo "Jorge Fletcher" is written: "En prueba de amistad a mi Sra Inés Camacho de Vallejo -- Calí a 2 de Decembre, 1864 Ch. A. Flétcher" 
Note: the name doesnt match picture title. "Ch. A. Flétcher" [CarlosAntonio Flétcher]  may be a sybling of Celina Flétcher [see Notes for Plate 4]
Note: The relationship of Joaquín Mesa to Inés Camacho is unknown.
Note: On the back of the "Joaquín Mesa" is written: "Mi Señora Ines: Es éste un recuerdo de gratitude i de cariño 
que le dedica su leal amigo. Joaquín López de Mesa? Calí Octubre 7, 1874." [the name Mesa? appeared to be "Mza" or Mya"
the name was right at the edge of the photo and was perhaps a slip of the pen]
